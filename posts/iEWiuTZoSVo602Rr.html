What Are Cases and How Do They Work?

Cases in CS2 are containers that can drop a weapon skin or rare items like knives or gloves. To open a case, you need to purchase a corresponding key (typically priced at $2.50). Once opened, the game runs an animation and gives you one random item from the case’s drop list.

Each case includes:

  • 8 common skins (from Consumer to Classified)

  • 1 rare (Covert) skin

  • A small chance of a "Special Item" — a knife or gloves

All skins come in various wear levels (Factory New, Minimal Wear, etc.).
The drop algorithm does not depend on your account, time of day, or how many cases you've opened — it’s pure RNG based on predefined odds.

When Should You Open Cases?

Timing isn't about luck — it's about strategy. While drops are completely random, market prices shift constantly. Here’s when it’s smart to open CS2 cases:

  • Before major sales: Open cases before big Steam discounts (summer and winter sales), so you can sell high before prices dip.

  • Right before a new case release: Older cases may spike in demand as collectors stock up.

  • After gameplay or graphic meta updates: Skins for newly popular weapons often increase in price.

How Many Cases to Open and Budgeting Tips

If you're opening for fun, set a clear limit. But if you're chasing profit, you’ll need some cold math. Here's the basic formula:

(Number of cases) × (price of key + price of case) = total risk

Example:
Opening 20 Fever Cases:

  • Cases: $0.67 × 20 = $13.40

  • Keys: $2.50 × 20 = $50.00

  • Total: $63.40

You’ll only break even or profit if you pull a pink, red, or knife skin.

Risk Management Tips:

  • Open in small batches of 5–10 cases
    This helps you gauge results without overspending.

  • Sell rare skins immediately
    Prices usually drop days after release.

  • Don’t open dozens at once
    There’s no such thing as a “warmed-up” account — odds stay the same.
