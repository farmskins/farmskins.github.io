Five-SeveN vs Tec-9 in CS2: Pistol Comparison

Despite their identical price tags, these pistols have different characteristics and in-game purposes. Five-SeveN: Stats and Playstyle

The Five-SeveN in CS2 is a CT-side pistol known for its accuracy, solid damage, and generous ammo reserve. It’s perfect for holding angles and playing a more controlled style of defense.

Key stats:

  • Body damage: 39

  • Headshot damage: 126

  • Fire rate: 400 RPM

  • Magazine: 20 rounds

  • Reserve ammo: 100 rounds

  • Price: $500

Pros:

  • High accuracy

  • Reliable in clutch situations

  • Large ammo pool

  • Solid armor penetration

Cons:

  • Lower fire rate

  • Less suited for aggressive pushes

The Five-SeveN shines in positional play, especially on medium-range engagements. It’s a great option when holding tight angles or countering force-buy rounds.

Tec-9: Stats and Playstyle

The CS2 Tec-9 is an aggressive T-side pistol built for rushes, quick entries, and close-quarters combat. It’s a popular pick during pistol and force rounds for its high burst potential.

Key stats:

  • Body damage: 41

  • Headshot damage: 132

  • Fire rate: 500 RPM

  • Magazine: 18 rounds

  • Reserve ammo: 90 rounds

  • Price: $500

Pros:

  • High fire rate

  • Effective while running

  • Great for fast entry plays

  • Excellent in close-range fights

Cons:

  • High spread

  • Harder to control

  • Lower total ammo

The Tec-9 CS2 enables terrorists to control the speed of the round, overpower defenders, and exploit swiftness and disorder.

Five-SeveN vs Tec-9: Head-to-Head Comparison

Let us section the two pistols' key differences so that you know how they perform in various situations.

Damage and Armor Penetration

Five-SeveN vs Tec-9. Both the guns can kill an unarmored opponent with a single headshot—126 with Five-SeveN and 132 with Tec-9. The Tec-9 causes a bit more body damage (41 as opposed to 39), but the Five-SeveN has a better killing rate against armored opponents as it carries higher armor penetration.

Fire Rate

The Tec-9 fires faster at 500 RPM, compared to 400 RPM on the Five-SeveN. This gives it an edge in close-range trades where every millisecond matters.

Accuracy and Spray Control

The Five-SeveN is more accurate and consistent, especially at medium range. The Tec-9, on the other hand, suffers from higher spread and is harder to control—though it’s much more effective while moving, which complements aggressive, fast-paced plays.

Magazine and Ammo

The CS2 Five-SeveN holds 20 rounds in the mag and has 100 in reserve, while the Tec-9 carries 18+90. While not a huge difference, the extra ammo on the Five-SeveN can come in handy during drawn-out rounds.

Playstyle and Usage

The Five-SeveN is a great choice for patient, disciplined players who hold angles and wait for engagements. The Tec-9 favors aggressive players who like to push, rush sites, and catch opponents off guard.

In short:

  • Five-SeveN is more stable, accurate, and ideal for CTs holding positions.

  • Tec-9 is a powerful tool for Ts looking to overwhelm the defense with speed and volume.

Which Pistol Should You Use in CS2?

It depends on your side and preferred playstyle. Here are some quick recommendations:

  • Playing CT and holding angles? Go for the Five-SeveN. It’s more accurate and consistent in defense.

  • T-side and planning a fast push? Pick the CS2 Tec-9. Its mobility and firepower shine in aggressive plays.

  • In clutch situations, the Five-SeveN often delivers better results thanks to its accuracy and control.

  • For fast trades and site entries, the Tec-9 might just be your best friend.

Key Differences Between Five-SeveN and Tec-9

The core differences lie in accuracy, fire rate, shooting mechanics, and intended use. The Five-SeveN is easier to control and performs better at medium range. The Tec-9 is more chaotic but excels in close-range fights and aggressive tactics.
