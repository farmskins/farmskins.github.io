Why Your CS2 FPS is Low? Here’s the Fix!

farmskins

With the transition to a new engine in CS2, the game received updated graphics and additional visual options. Every player starting their journey in CS2 is likely to encounter challenges when configuring their PC, especially graphic settings. While the new engine introduced various improvements, it also brought performance issues, particularly on lower-end systems—even when using optimal settings.When your PC is struggling to manage advanced video settings, it can have a massive impact on how the game runs. The most frequently encountered problem is the reduction of CS2 FPS, which often results in stuttering graphics and an overall aggravating gaming session - which is not ideal.

Delving deeper, in this article we will pinpoint all of the contributing factors of low FPS, as well as present some great solutions to improve the performance of your game.

Main Causes of Low FPS

A CS2 FPS drop usually means some of the components of your computer are failing to work and keep up with the demands of the game paired with the settings you have selected. Compared to CS:GO, CS2 has significantly more demanding system requirements for it to run smoothly, especially on medium or high graphics. A deficiency in hardware is not the only contributing factor. Lagging internet or malfunctioning servers can equally affect game performance.Let’s explore how to get to the bottom of the issue and resolve it.

How to Check Your FPS in CS2

Before optimizing your FPS, you need to know how low it actually is. To display your current FPS on screen:

  • Open the developer console by pressing the tilde key ~.

  • Type the following command:

cl_showfps 1

This will show your FPS in the top-left corner of the screen. To hide it again, use:

cl_showfps 0

Recommended System Requirements for CS2

For a smooth experience in CS2, a capable PC is essential. After major updates, system requirements often increase, and CS2 is no exception. Here are the recommended specs for optimal performance:

  • OS: Windows 10 64-bit

  • CPU: Quad-core, 8-thread Intel or AMD processor

  • RAM: 8 GB

  • GPU: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 970 or AMD Radeon RX 570

  • DirectX: Version 11

  • Storage: 60 GB free space

If your PC doesn’t meet these specs, the game also offers minimum system requirements to run at a lower quality:

  • OS: Windows 10 64-bit

  • CPU: Dual-core, 4-thread Intel or AMD processor @ 2.8 GHz

  • RAM: 4 GB

  • GPU: GeForce GTX 650 or Radeon HD 7750

  • Storage: 60 GB

While these specs allow CS2 to launch, you won't experience its enhanced graphics or full performance potential.

Optimal Graphics Settings to Boost FPS

To improve performance without sacrificing too much visual quality, try these CS2 settings for FPS:

  • Player Contrast: On – Enhances visibility

  • Vertical Sync: Off – Reduces input lag and improves FPS

  • Anti-Aliasing: 4x MSAA – Smooths edges without major performance loss

  • Shadow Quality: High – Helps spot enemies behind cover

  • Model & Texture Detail: Low – Increases FPS with minimal visual compromise

  • Shader Detail: Low – Slightly affects lighting and shadows

  • Particle Detail: Low – Reduces clutter during action

  • Global Shadowing: Off – Major FPS gain with little visual impact

  • HDR: Performance – Balances brightness and performance

  • NVIDIA Reflex Low Latency: On + Boost – Reduces input delay

  • FidelityFX Super Resolution: Performance – Increases FPS without lowering perceived quality

Adjust these settings as needed for your specific system to find the best balance.

Top 5 Ways to Improve FPS in CS2

Let’s highlight five of the most effective methods for increasing FPS:

Use a Config File

A config file contains custom settings, including commands that can improve FPS on powerful systems. You can create your own or download a professional player's config.

How to install:

  • Download and unzip the config file.

  • Rename it to autoexec.cfg if needed.

  • Move it to:

C:\Program Files (x86)\Steam\steamapps\common\Counter-Strike Global Offensive\game\csgo\cfg

  • In launch options, add: +exec autoexec.cfg

You can always reset settings in-game if needed.

Enable AMD FidelityFX Super Resolution

This technology upscales lower-resolution CS2 frames to improve performance without reducing visual quality.

How to enable:

  • Open CS2

  • Go to Settings > Video > Advanced Video

  • Find and enable FidelityFX Super Resolution

Optimize NVIDIA Control Panel Settings

You can tweak your graphics card settings to CS2 boost FPS:

Steps:

  • Right-click on desktop → NVIDIA Control Panel

  • Go to Manage 3D Settings

  • Under Program Settings, add CS2

Recommended settings:

  • Image Sharpening: Off

  • Ambient Occlusion: Off

  • Anisotropic Filtering: Application-controlled

  • Anti-Aliasing: Off

  • Max Frame Rate: Off

  • Low Latency Mode: Ultra

  • Power Management Mode: Prefer maximum performance

  • Shader Cache Size: Unlimited

  • Texture Filtering: High Performance

  • Triple Buffering: Off

Make sure to test changes to see what works best for your system.

Use Console Commands

First, enable the developer console in settings. Then use these commands:

  • cl_showfps 1 – Displays FPS

  • net_graph 1 – Shows detailed performance stats

  • fps_max 0 – Removes FPS cap

  • sys_info – Displays system info and applied commands

Tweak In-Game Settings

In CS2, go to Settings → Video → Graphics and set:

  • Display Mode: Fullscreen

  • Power Saving Mode: Off

In the Advanced Settings tab:

  • Player Contrast: On

  • V-Sync: Off

  • Multisampling AA Mode: CMAA2

  • Shadow Quality: High

  • Model/Texture/Shader/Particle Detail: Low

  • HDR: Quality

  • FidelityFX Super Resolution: Quality

  • NVIDIA Reflex Low Latency: On

These settings are just suggestions—feel free to adjust based on your hardware.

Launch Options to Boost CS2 Performance

If nothing else works, try modifying your Steam launch options. Right-click CS2 in your library → Properties → General → Launch Options and add the following:

-nojoy -high +fps_max 0 +mat_queue_mode 2 -d3d9ex cl_allow_animated_avatars 0 +r_dynamic 0 +engine_low_latency_sleep_after_client_tick

What these do:

  • -nojoy: Disables joystick support

  • -high: Runs the game with high CPU priority

  • +fps_max 0: Removes FPS cap

  • +mat_queue_mode 2: Enables multithreaded rendering

  • -d3d9ex: Improves window switching performance

  • cl_allow_animated_avatars 0: Reduces GPU load

  • +r_dynamic 0: Disables dynamic lighting

  • +engine_low_latency_sleep_after_client_tick: Reduces stutter

Remember, every PC is different, so not every tip will work equally for everyone. Try out these methods and find what suits your system best for a smoother and more enjoyable CS2 experience.
