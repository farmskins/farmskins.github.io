Types of Strafes

Before diving into how to strafe in CS2, let’s start from the basics – the essence of strafing.

In CS2, strafing refers to moving the player’s model from side to side. Its primary purpose is to make the player a harder target for the enemy while simultaneously increasing their own accuracy. There are several types of strafes, each with its own features and applications in different situations.

Basic Strafe

The basic strafe is the most fundamental movement, where the player moves left or right using the A and D keys. This strafe allows you to quickly change direction and dodge enemy fire. Players often combine it with crouching, further increasing the chances of avoiding bullets.

Diagonal Strafe

A diagonal strafe is used for more precise positioning and changes in movement direction. The player’s model moves at an angle when holding two keys simultaneously, such as A and W. This strafe is particularly useful for getting into advantageous positions.

Air Strafe

An air strafe is performed in mid-air and helps control the direction of a jump. To execute this maneuver, release all movement keys and use the mouse to adjust direction. Air strafing can be used for long jumps or to overcome obstacles on the map.

Circular Strafe

Circular strafing is used to circle around an opponent or an object. To perform this, hold a movement key in one direction (e.g., A) while simultaneously moving the mouse in the same direction. This type of strafe is effective for dodging shots and gaining positional advantage.

Strafing and Counter-Strafing

The technique that allows you to stop quickly after strafing is called CS2 counter-strafing. For instance, if you are strafing to the right (holding D) and then quickly press the opposite key (in this case, A), your character will come to an immediate stop.

Mastering counter-strafing is essential because the inertia from movement throws off your weapon’s aim, reducing shooting accuracy. Counter-strafing eliminates this problem entirely.

Strafing and counter-strafing are closely related and are often used together. You can strafe left or right to dodge bullets while approaching an enemy, counter-strafe to stop and fire accurately, and then resume strafing to keep moving. This tactic makes you a difficult target while allowing you to maintain precise aim.

Strafing for Positioning

Practicing strafing can help players move quickly and safely across the map, avoid open spaces, and minimize exposure to enemy fire.

Here are some ways strafing can be useful:

  1. Quick movement between cover: Players can move swiftly between cover, making themselves harder to hit, especially when crossing open areas.

  2. Dodging enemy bullets: Constantly changing direction makes it harder for opponents to aim accurately. Fast and unpredictable strafes make you less vulnerable to enemy shots.

  3. Reaching high positions: Air strafing is invaluable when jumping onto high platforms or overcoming obstacles, allowing you to control your jump direction and land precisely.

  4. Flanking opponents: Circular strafing allows you to flank enemies and secure advantageous positions for surprise attacks, especially in close combat or tight spaces.

  5. Capturing key map points: Strafing helps players quickly reach bomb plant sites or other important areas, allowing your team to control the flow of the game.

How to Master Movement in CS2

To truly understand how to strafe in CS2, instructional videos on YouTube can be incredibly helpful. In these, experienced players share tips, techniques, and demonstrate how to move efficiently in CS2 to outmaneuver opponents.

However, the only way to significantly improve your gameplay is through dedicated practice. Steam Workshop even offers specific maps for this purpose:

  • Aim Botz

  • Bot Training

  • AIM & MOVEMENT

Train on these maps against bots to get accustomed to the game’s dynamics and improve your reaction time. Once you’ve built some confidence, move on to practicing in real matches.

Don’t be discouraged if, after watching tutorials on strafing in CS2, you feel far from mastering the technique. The key to success lies in consistent practice. Regular training will undoubtedly improve your movement skills and make your gameplay smoother and more precise.

Now you know how vital the movement mechanics in CS2 are, what strafing is, and how to execute basic and advanced strafes. Watch a few tutorials on counter-strafing in CS2, then start practicing. Mastering movement will give you a significant advantage in matches, improving your odds of victory.
