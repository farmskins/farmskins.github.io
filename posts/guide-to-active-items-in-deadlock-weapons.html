Guide to Active Items in Deadlock: Weapons

farmskins

The world of Deadlock is incredibly diverse, with each character possessing unique abilities that require a tailored approach. However, in this universe, not only character abilities matter—active items, which can be purchased in the shop, play a significant role. These items open up new tactical opportunities and often determine the outcome of battles.

It's essential to consider the active items in both your allies' and enemies' inventories, as they can greatly influence the battle. So, let's take a closer look at the active items in Deadlock in the "Weapons" category and how using them wisely can give you an advantage.

Low-Cost Active Items

Fleetfoot

In the early laning phase, before the 10th minute, there are no active items in the shop priced lower than 1000 souls, and the only option at this stage is Fleetfoot.

This item increases the range of dashes, making it perfect for aggressive play on the lane or in combat. It boosts the character's survivability by increasing health and removes the movement speed penalty. Upon activation, Fleetfoot provides temporary extra mobility and increases potential damage to enemies. Therefore, this item is useful for both pressuring on the lane and for an aggressive playstyle in teamfights.

Mid-Cost Active Items

In the mid-game, more expensive artifacts priced at 3000 souls appear. At this point, heroes usually have access to their ultimate abilities, and the primary goal becomes dealing consistent damage while staying in the thick of battle. Items in this category help with survivability and avoiding damage while also enhancing offensive capabilities.

Alchemical Fire

This artifact focuses on increasing damage, both physical and magical. An additional bonus is the improved bullet shield, which boosts survivability. The active ability allows you to throw flasks that deal area damage. To get the most out of this item, it's important to hit the enemy's formations with the flask and slow their movement, which enables you to deal significant damage.

Heroic Aura

This Deadlock item is more oriented toward summoners. In addition to increasing your own attack speed, it also boosts the attack speed of summoned creatures. This makes it an ideal choice for heroes like McGinnis, who specializes in summoning turrets. However, if your build does not involve using summons, this item’s effectiveness will be limited.

Warp Stone

The Warp Stone provides teleportation, making it an excellent choice for escaping or quickly repositioning in battle. It can be a lifesaver when you need to quickly exit a dangerous area. It's especially useful for heroes like Infernus, who often find themselves in the center of fights and need a way to quickly disengage.

High-Cost Active Items

In the late game, where every mistake can cost you the victory, high-cost items play a crucial role. These artifacts can significantly affect the outcome of battles, especially if used at critical moments.

Silencer

One of the most expensive items in the game, Silencer costs 7550 souls and provides a powerful disable that works through basic attacks. This item greatly increases physical damage while reducing the enemy's ability to use magical abilities. It’s effective for both initiating fights and retreating. To craft it, you will first need to obtain the "Slowing Bullets." Silencer is especially useful against magic-heavy enemies, as it allows you to control their actions in battle.

Vampiric Burst

This item costs 6300 souls and further increases physical damage while boosting the character's movement speed. It is targeted at heroes who face "tanky" opponents. Thanks to its lifesteal effect, Vampiric Burst allows you to effectively sustain in prolonged fights by maintaining high survivability and dealing constant damage. The additional bonuses to ammo and attack speed make it an excellent choice for heroes specializing in continuous damage output.

Conclusion

In this guide, we’ve covered all the active items from the "Weapons" in Deadlock, each of which focuses on increasing physical damage. Most of these items improve the aggressive qualities of a character and enhance their survivability in battle, making them an essential part of a carry build. However, as with all aspects of Deadlock, the key to victory lies in the proper use of items, depending on the map situation and your opponents' actions.
