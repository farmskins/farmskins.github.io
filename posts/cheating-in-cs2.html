Type of cheats

  1. WallHacks: This CS2 cheat allows players to see through walls and obstacles, giving them an unfair advantage by revealing enemy positions. Players using WallHacks can anticipate movements and pre-fire at opponents who would otherwise be hidden from view.

  2. AimBots: AimBot cheats automatically snap a player’s crosshair to an enemy’s head, making every shot incredibly accurate, even through walls or smoke. This is often visible through unnatural aim movements and suspiciously high headshot percentages.

  3. Radar Hacks: These cheats provide additional information on the player’s radar, showing enemy positions and movements that should be hidden. This allows the cheater to make strategic decisions without obtaining legitimate in-game information.

  4. SpinBotting: SpinBot makes a player rapidly rotate, making them nearly impossible to hit while still being able to shoot at opponents with precision. This cheat is most commonly seen in blatant hacks.

The impact of cheating on players

Cheating has a profound impact on the competitive CS2 scene, where fairness and skill are paramount. For example, high-level players like streamers have reported encountering cheaters in nearly every Premier mode match, significantly undermining the competitive experience. Even in professional settings, cheating scandals have occurred, such as the case of KQLY, a pro player banned for using cheats during his career. This erodes trust in the system, forcing many to rely on platforms like FACEIT to ensure fair play.

Current Anti-Cheat Measures

  1. Valve’s VAC System: VAC can detect and ban players using known cheats, but many cheaters bypass the system, continuing to ruin matches. One positive feature is the VAC Live system, which can cancel matches if a cheater is detected during the game.

How does VAC work?

  1. Ban Waves: Valve occasionally conducts large-scale ban waves, targeting players using specific cheats. These waves have been somewhat effective, temporarily reducing the number of cheaters in the game.

  2. FACEIT:  FACEIT offers a more robust anti-cheat system for competitive players. With stricter account verification and proactive cheat detection, FACEIT has become a popular platform for players seeking fairer competition. It is particularly effective at preventing cheaters from disrupting high-level matches.

  3. Player Reporting System: CS2 relies on the community to report suspicious behavior. Players can use the in-game reporting feature to flag cheaters, helping Valve investigate and ban offenders. While this system is useful, it can be slow, and false reports can sometimes clutter the process.

Community and Developer Response

The CS2 community has been vocal about the growing issue of cheating, with many players reporting frequent encounters with cheaters in competitive modes. Streamer dima_wallhacks, for example, reported running into 81 cheaters out of 100 Premier matches, which ignited outrage among players who feel Valve’s efforts are insufficient. Additionally, professional players like ropz have expressed frustrations about the prevalence of cheating, calling for stronger measures.

In response, Valve has implemented ban waves and introduced VAC Live, which cancels matches if a cheater is detected mid-game.

Future of Anti-Cheat in CS2

The future of anti-cheat in CS2 likely involves adopting kernel-level systems, similar to those in other games, to detect cheats more effectively at a deeper level. Although this raises privacy concerns, it could significantly reduce bypassing attempts. Integration with third-party platforms like FACEIT, known for stronger anti-cheat measures, could also offer a safer competitive environment. Real-time detection, such as VAC Live, which cancels matches when cheaters are detected, needs further refinement to address the evolving cheat landscape. To succeed, Valve must ensure faster updates and better communication with the community.
