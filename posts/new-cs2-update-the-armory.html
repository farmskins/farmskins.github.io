New CS2 Update: The Armory

farmskins

The recent CS2 update introduced one of the largest content expansions to date, featuring the launch of "The Armory" event. This event, viewed as a replacement for previous operations, eliminates missions and reward medals. Instead, players can purchase a pass, earn experience in the game, and spend event points in the shop.

New Cosmetics: Charms and Weapon Collections

One of the most anticipated features is the introduction of CS2 weapon charms. Players now have access to two collections of these cosmetic items. Charms, a long-discussed customization element in the community, have been met with mixed reactions despite their expected addition.

Additionally, three new CS2 weapon collections have been introduced, including skins for the updated Overpass map. Players can also find two new sticker collections and a new case with unique items, including the Kukri knife. Interestingly, a previously discovered "Twin Blade" knife was not included in this update, likely to extend the release of content.

Items Available in the "Armory Pass"

  • "Connecting Link" charm collection (3 credits for a random charm)

  • "Small Caliber" charm collection (3 credits)

  • Overpass 2024 skin collection (4 credits)

  • "Graphic" skin collection (4 credits)

  • "Sports and Field" skin collection (4 credits)

  • Gallery Case (2 credits for the case, key sold separately)

  • "Collect the Elements" sticker collection (1 credit)

  • "Collect the Figure" sticker collection (1 credit)

  • Limited Desert Eagle "Heat Treatment" (25 credits, available until January 14, 2025)

The "Armory Pass" might serve as a replacement for traditional operations. All CS2 items from the pass or acquired via trade or the marketplace are now subject to a week-long trade restriction.

New and Improved Features

Other notable updates include the return of the StatTrak mechanic, which tracks weapon usage statistics, and the ability to adjust sticker wear levels before applying them. Players can now also detach charms from weapons, though this comes at a cost.

Technical Changes and Bugs

In addition to cosmetic CS2 updates, the patch brings various gameplay improvements. For example, an issue with character leg movement that caused players to see characters mimicking Michael Jackson's "dance moves" was fixed. Telemetry graphics for server connection tracking were also improved, alongside minor menu refinements.

As is often the case with major updates, bugs have surfaced, and developers are expected to address them in the coming months. However, players can look forward to an exciting period filled with new features and patches.

Hidden Additions and Developer Plans

File analysis reveals that chickens in CS2 can now blink, and new idle animations for player models were added. These changes might hint at future plans to introduce pets into the game. Additionally, traces of a new charm inspection animation were found, suggesting that the development team is continuing to enhance customization options.

Overall, the "Armory" CS2 update has brought a wealth of new content, promising to make the game even more diverse and engaging, despite the technical issues that still need to be addressed.
