All Glove Types in CS2: The Ultimate Skin Guide

farmskins

Gloves in CS2 are more than just a cosmetic accessory. They’ve become an essential part of a player’s personal style—especially for those building visually cohesive and striking inventories. Unlike weapon skins, glove skins are rare, expensive, and come from limited sources—cases, capsules, or the Steam Market.
In this guide, we’ll break down every glove type in CS2, their unique features, looks, rarity, and how to choose the perfect pair to match your playstyle.

Why Do Gloves Matter in CS2?

Gloves CS2 offer no tactical advantage. They won’t improve your aim, reduce recoil, or make you move faster. However, in the world of collectors and inventory aesthetes, gloves are among the most coveted skins in the game. They decorate your hands in first-person view and pair beautifully with knives and weapons, helping you build a cohesive visual theme.

Sport Gloves

Sport CS2 Gloves feature bold, aggressive, and modern designs. Crafted from artificial fibers, decorative textile details, and bright neon embellishments, these gloves are favored by gamers who like a lively, attention-grabbing look.They shout swiftness and assurance—ideal for gamers who aim to be noticeable on the platform. The stylish, fast-looking design makes them feel like they fit in a cyberpunk or futuristic sports venue. Whether you’re in a hurry or maintaining a position, these gloves exude a bold, showy attitude.

Popular skins:

  • Pandora’s Box – A purple and black design with neon details.

  • Vice – Neon pink and blue for a flashy, Miami-style vibe.

  • Slingshot – Black and red with palm-side accents.

Sport Gloves are among the most expensive and sought-after glove types, pairing well with colorful knives like Doppler, Fade, or Tiger Tooth.

Specialist Gloves

Specialist CS2 Gloves resemble military-grade gear with leather patches, rubberized areas, and reinforced knuckles. They have a serious, tactical aesthetic.
These gloves look like they belong to a spec-ops unit—built for rugged combat environments. Their design prioritizes protection and utility, reflecting a disciplined, no-nonsense approach. Ideal for players who enjoy realistic skins and military-style loadouts.

Popular skins:

  • Crimson Kimono – Red and black textures with Japanese-inspired motifs.

  • Fade – A shimmering gradient of purple and yellow.

  • Field Agent – A muted grey-orange camouflage look.

Perfect for players who favor military-style inventories. These gloves pair nicely with knives like the M9 Bayonet, Karambit, or Ursus.

Driver Gloves

Driver Gloves CS2 represent grace and traditional design. Crafted from high-quality leather, they look like genuine driving gloves with simple patterns and polished sewing.There’s a classic quality to Driver Gloves—they seem as if they were from a high-end vehicle’s glove box. With a classic yet stylish touch, they are perfect for players who prefer understated skins rather than bold patterns. The surface and intricacies imply elegance and refinement rather than unfiltered force.

Popular skins:

  • King Snake – White gloves with grey snake-scale textures.

  • Imperial Plaid – Dark brown leather with a plaid upper.

  • Crimson Weave – Deep red with black trim.

Ideal for those who appreciate understated sophistication. Best matched with classic knives like the Bayonet, Flip Knife, or Classic Knife.

Moto Gloves

Moto Gloves CS2 are bulky, tough-looking gloves inspired by biker gear. With thick padding, rubberized elements, and abrasion-resistant leather, they have a rugged, intimidating appearance.They evoke images of steel, engines, and danger. The armor-like structure of Moto Gloves makes them feel like battle gear, worn by players who dive headfirst into fights. They offer a raw, mechanical vibe and command attention in-game thanks to their size and silhouette.

Popular skins:

  • Spearmint – Mint green and white, popular among collectors.

  • Blood Pressure – Black and red for an aggressive, intense look.

  • Cool Mint – Light blue and grey for a colder, steel-toned style.

These gloves pair especially well with larger knives like the Bowie Knife, Survival Knife, or Huntsman Knife.

Hand Wraps

Hand Wraps CS2 aren’t traditional gloves—they’re cloth or leather wraps like those worn by MMA fighters. They offer a raw, aggressive look and stand out from the crowd.
These wraps feel primal and brutal, perfect for players who want their style to reflect fierce, unrefined combat. With visible stitching and worn textures, Hand Wraps look like they’ve been through countless fights. They add an edge of ferocity to any loadout.

Popular skins:

  • Cobalt Skulls – Dark blue with skull prints.

  • Slaughter – Red wraps with the same pattern as the Slaughter knife.

  • Overprint – Graffiti-style design on grey fabric.

Valued for their uniqueness, Hand Wraps are a great choice for players who want to stand out. They work especially well with flashy or savage-looking knives.

Operation-Linked Gloves

Some gloves in CS2 are tied to specific operations. These are only available through cases released during that operation, making them rare and collectible.
Operation gloves often feature distinctive designs and lore-based themes linked to the operation’s aesthetic. Because they are limited to a specific release window, they hold long-term collector value. Many players seek these gloves for their exclusivity and story-based appeal.

Examples:

  • Broken Fang Gloves | Jade – A green camo style from Operation Broken Fang.

  • Hydra Gloves | Rattler – Snake-skin texture and tactical elements.

  • Bloodhound Gloves | Bronzed – Metallic styling with the Bloodhound logo.

Key features:

  • Limited-time drops make these gloves highly valuable on the secondary market.

Wear Levels and Rarity

Wear levels:

  • Factory New (FN)

  • Minimal Wear (MW)

  • Field-Tested (FT)

  • Well-Worn (WW)

  • Battle-Scarred (BS)

Rarity:

  • All gloves are either:

Classified (purple)

Covert (red)

The better the condition and the rarer the skin, the higher the price. Some models can fetch over $5,000 on the Steam Market.

How to Get Gloves in CS2

  • Through the Farmskins website.

  • Opening cases – Extremely low drop chance (~0.25%). In cases such as the Gloves Case, Covert Gloves, Clutch Case, and Revolution.

  • Steam Market purchase – The most reliable way to get the exact model you want.

  • Player trades – Through third-party sites or direct trades via Steam.

How to Choose Gloves That Fit Your Style

  • Match with your knife – A perfect pair creates a cohesive look. For example, Sport Gloves | Pandora’s Box look great with Butterfly Knife | Doppler.

  • Coordinate with your weapons – Align glove colors with your AWP, AK-47, M4A4, etc.

  • Consider your aesthetic:

Driver Gloves – for fans of minimalism

Moto Gloves – for aggressive players

Hand Wraps – for those who like to stand out

Conclusion

Each glove type in CS2 offers a unique visual flair. Whether your taste leans toward sporty flash, tactical minimalism, or exotic aggression, there’s a perfect pair of CS2 gloves to elevate your loadout. Gloves are an investment in your personal style, your in-game presence, and your overall enjoyment of CS2.
