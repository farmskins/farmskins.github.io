The Most Popular Devices Among CS2 Esports Players: Is It Worth the Extra Cost?

farmskins

Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) is one of the most popular FPS games, requiring maximum focus, quick reflexes, and precision from its players. It’s no surprise that many aspiring gamers wonder if investing in high-end devices is necessary for success. But are these investments really justified, or can you get by with more budget-friendly options? Let's explore the preferences of professional CS2 esports players and whether it's worth following their lead.

Monitors: The Hertz Difference Is Real

Pro Player Preferences:

  • ZOWIE XL2546K 240Hz — 45%

  • ZOWIE XL2566K 360Hz — 40%

  • Other models (ZOWIE, ASUS ROG, etc.) — 15%

The advantages of high-refresh-rate monitors are clear: the higher the refresh rate (Hz), the more frames are displayed on the screen every second. This directly impacts the smoothness of the picture, which is especially important in fast-paced games like CS2. Most pro players have already switched to monitors with 240Hz or 360Hz refresh rates, allowing them to see and react to events faster than opponents using more budget-friendly screens.

However, for the average player, buying a monitor with a refresh rate higher than 144Hz might be an unnecessary luxury. Yes, the difference between 60Hz and 144Hz is significant, but the gap between 240Hz and 360Hz is less noticeable. If you're on a budget, upgrading to a 144Hz monitor is already a considerable step forward.

Keyboards: Control at a New Level

Pro Player Preferences:

  • Logitech G715 — 30%

  • Razer Huntsman V3 Pro — 25%

  • Logitech G Pro X — 20%

  • HyperX Alloy FPS — 10%

  • SteelSeries Apex Pro — 10%

Mechanical keyboards have long been the standard among gamers due to their responsiveness and reliability. They allow better control over keystrokes, which can be a decisive factor in dynamic moments. Notably, the Razer Huntsman V3 Pro keyboard, with its Snap Tap technology, stands out from the competition, enabling instant switching between actions—a feature particularly useful for strafing in CS2.

But is it worth the price? If you're serious about esports and striving for perfection, perhaps it is. However, for most players who aren't aiming for a professional level, any quality mechanical CS2 keyboard with proven switches (red, blue, brown) will suffice.

Mice: Sensors and Response Time

Pro Player Preferences:

  • Logitech G Pro X Superlight 1 or 2 — 40%

  • ZOWIE EC2-CW — 27%

  • Razer DeathAdder V3 Pro — 23%

  • VAXEE, SteelSeries, others — 10%

A mouse is an extension of your hand in the game. High-quality models provide precise and reliable tracking of movements, which is crucial for aiming and shooting. However, when choosing a mouse, you shouldn’t base your decision solely on comfort. Other important factors include sensor quality, response time, and connection type (wired or wireless).

If your budget allows, consider flagship wireless models like the Logitech G Pro X Superlight. However, wired mice are still an excellent choice, especially if you need to save some money.

Headphones: A Noticeable Difference or Just a Trendy Accessory?

Pro Player Preferences:

  • HyperX Cloud II — 40%

  • Logitech G Pro X Headset — 40%

  • Razer BlackShark V2 Pro — 15%

Sound plays a key role in CS2: the ability to accurately pinpoint an opponent’s footsteps or the direction of gunfire can give you a crucial advantage. Pros often choose high-quality headsets, but it’s worth noting that many prefer in-ear monitors (IEMs), especially at LAN tournaments. These offer excellent sound and comfort, often at a more affordable price.

For home use, consider purchasing in-ear monitors, which, when paired with a good microphone, can be a more cost-effective and convenient solution than a full headset.

Conclusion: Is It Worth the Extra Cost?

Investing in high-end devices makes sense for those aiming for a professional level of play. High-quality monitors, keyboards, mice, and headphones can indeed provide a small but significant advantage. However, for most players who aren’t competing for CS2 esports titles, budget-friendly solutions can also deliver a decent level of comfort and performance. The key is to understand your needs and choose devices accordingly.
