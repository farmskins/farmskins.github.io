What Map Will Be Added Next to CS2? Possible Future Developments

farmskins

In the Counter-Strike community, rumors and expectations are always in the air, especially when it comes to adding new maps to the game. The question of which map Valve will introduce to the next CS2 map pool is of great interest to gamers. However, based on the current situation, the wait may be prolonged.

The Story of Dust2: From a Dusty Past to a Counter-Strike Legend

Valve and Map Updates: Should We Expect Anything New?

Looking at the history of map pool changes, it's clear that Valve tends to update maps only once a year. Exceptions were made only in 2020 when the pandemic interfered. In 2023, an update already took place: in April, the enhanced Overpass was removed, and the classic Dust2 was brought back. Thus, the next update is likely to come in early 2025.

Train – The Leading Contender for a Comeback

Among all the CS2 maps, Train seems to be the most obvious candidate for a return. Valve has already showcased an updated version of this map in a CS2 teaser, giving fans plenty to speculate about. In fact, developers love to leave “Easter eggs,” but in this case, it’s not just a hint—it’s almost an official confirmation.

The story of Train's remake dates back to 2023, when textures for the updated map were found in the game’s code. However, the current stage of its development remains a mystery.

The Forgotten Legend: Tuscan

Tuscan is a legendary CS2 map from CS 1.6, which made its way back to CSGO only in August 2022. Valve bought the rights to the map from map makers Catfood and Brute, delighting long-time fans. However, the question remains: will Tuscan be included in the CS2 map pool, or will it be forgotten again? It's hard to say, especially given that Valve’s actions don't always follow a logical pattern.

Cache: A Map in Development, But No Guarantees

Fmpone, the creator of Cache, has been actively sharing news about the remake of the map. The new version promises to retain elements of the classic Cache, while also incorporating changes from the revised green version. However, there’s a catch: Valve may not want to buy the map, casting doubt on its appearance in the official map pool.

Furthermore, Fmpone has previously voiced dissatisfaction with Valve’s policy regarding community-created maps. He sharply criticized the company’s approach, arguing that it limits the game’s development. Despite these differences, the chances of seeing Cache in CS2 are still high, but whether it will be used in tournaments remains uncertain.

The Return of Cobblestone: A Winter Wonderland?

Cobblestone is another iconic map that was removed from the pool in 2018 after an unsuccessful rework. However, rumors of its return in CS2 persist. Moreover, there’s talk that the map might come back with a winter version, which would be an exciting addition to the competitive scene. However, even this doesn’t guarantee Cobblestone’s inclusion in official tournaments.

Some leaks from game files suggest that Valve might release a new map based on Cobblestone, rather than just a remake of the old version. This only adds to the intrigue.

The Possibility of a New Map

In addition to the return of old favorites, there’s a chance that Valve could introduce an entirely new CS2 map that no one has seen before. In 2022, Dust2 was removed from the map pool, and Anubis was added, which came as a surprise. A similar scenario could happen in the next update.

Valve may also release a new operation, which would provide a reason to introduce new maps. In any case, since no operations have been released for CS2 yet, expecting a "new-old" location might be premature.

Pistols in Counter-Strike: History, Impact, and Balance

The Game Changes, But Vertigo Remains

While players debate which map in CS2 should return, many agree on one thing: Vertigo, according to most, should be removed from the map pool. Although CS2 is nearing its first anniversary, and no new 5v5 map has been added so far, the focus is not just on bringing back Train, Cobblestone, or Cache, but on finally saying goodbye to Vertigo, which remains unpopular among a significant portion of the community.

Counter-Strike 2 continues to evolve, and the gaming community eagerly awaits new updates. Whatever Valve's next steps may be, one thing is certain: interest in the game will remain strong, and discussions about maps will continue.
