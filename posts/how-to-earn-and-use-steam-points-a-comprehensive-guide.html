How to Earn and Use Steam Points: A Comprehensive Guide

farmskins

Steam is the largest platform for digital game distribution, and one of its unique features is the Steam Points system. This virtual currency allows players to customize their profiles, purchase various items, and even earn real money. In this guide, we will delve into how to use Steam Points and accumulate them, as well as various strategies to help you get the most out of this system.

What Are Steam Points?

Steam Points are a special currency awarded to users for purchasing games or in-game items on the Steam platform. Points can be used to buy various cosmetic items such as animated backgrounds, avatars, stickers, and even complete game profiles. This currency allows players to personalize their profiles and stand out among other users.

However, Steam Points have more potential than they seem at first glance. In addition to their official use in the Steam store, they can also serve as a virtual currency in unofficial exchanges between users. For example, you can agree with another player to transfer points in exchange for a product or service, but keep in mind that such deals are not regulated by Valve and can be risky.

Ways to Earn Steam Points

1. Purchasing Games and In-Game Items

The most obvious way to earn Steam Points is by purchasing games and in-game items from the official Steam store. For every dollar spent, you earn a certain number of points. The standard rate is 100 points for every dollar spent. So, if you buy a game for $5, you’ll receive 500 points. Similarly, if you purchase an in-game item for 25 cents, you'll get 25 points.

However, it’s important to note that not all spending results in points. For example, purchases on the Steam Marketplace, where users trade items, do not earn additional points. Therefore, if your goal is to accumulate points, it's better to focus on purchases in the Steam store and in-game shops rather than on the marketplace.

2. Point Farming Through Reselling In-Game Items

This method requires a bit more effort but can be quite profitable with the right approach. The basic idea is to buy in-game items and immediately resell them, thus returning the money to your balance while simultaneously accumulating points.

For example, buying items in games like Dota 2 or Team Fortress 2 allows you to immediately list them for sale on the marketplace. However, keep in mind that in some games, such as CS2, there is a week-long delay before you can sell items.

When selecting items for resale, you need to carefully consider Steam's commission. The standard commission is 13%, so you need to sell items at a markup to avoid losses. A good example of a successful resale item is the "Flashbang Sticker." You can buy it for 99 cents and sell it on the marketplace for $1.35. After deducting the commission, you will receive $1.17, giving you a net profit of 18 cents per sale.

3. Free Points from Other Players

Another on way how to get Steam Points is through rewards from other players. Steam allows users to reward each other for helpful guides, screenshots, illustrations, game reviews, and other types of content. If your content is useful or interesting to the community, players can thank you by sending you a certain number of points.

However, there are some nuances here. When transferring rewards, you lose 2/3 of the points due to commission. For example, if you are awarded 600 points, you will receive only 200 points. Additionally, rewards do not come immediately but after 2 weeks, which can also complicate the process of accumulating points.

Despite this, receiving rewards is an excellent way to accumulate points for free, especially if you are actively involved in the community and create quality content.

4. Buying Points from Other Players

There are special platforms, such as Funpay, where users can buy and sell Steam Points. This method is the simplest and fastest, but it also carries risks. When buying points from other players, it’s essential to choose verified sellers with good reviews and to use a guarantor service to protect yourself from potential fraud.

The process of purchasing points through such platforms typically works as follows: you choose a seller, agree on a price and method of point transfer, and after completing the deal, receive your points. However, it’s important to note that the transfer of points may take up to 14 days, as sellers often use the reward system to transfer points.

This method is especially useful for those who want to quickly top up their point balance without spending time on purchasing and reselling in-game items.

5. Earning Money by Selling Points

If you want not only to accumulate points but also to earn money from them, you can become a seller on platforms like Funpay. The principle is simple: you farm points and then sell them to other users for real money. However, keep in mind that you will bear the entire commission, and to transfer 1,000 points, you will need to send 3,000 points due to commissions.

This method of earning requires some effort and patience, but it can be quite profitable, especially if you already have a steady source of point farming.

How to Earn from Reselling In-Game Items?

Now let’s take a closer look at the process of earning money by reselling in-game items. As mentioned earlier, a good example of a successful item is the "Flashbang Sticker." When you buy it for 99 cents and sell it for $1.35, after deducting the commission, you will receive $1.17. This gives you a net profit of 18 cents per sale.

If you buy and sell 100 such stickers, your total profit will be $17. Of course, this amount may seem small, but if you are actively engaged in reselling, these amounts can add up to significant profits.

However, it’s important to remember that the prices of in-game items can fluctuate, and what is a profitable deal today may become unprofitable tomorrow. Therefore, always keep an eye on market prices and try to find the most advantageous deals.

Conclusion

Using Steam Points opens up many opportunities for players. You can not only decorate your profile but also earn money by reselling items or selling points to other players. However, to make the most of this system, it’s essential to understand all the nuances, including Steam’s commission, reward transfer time, and the risks associated with unofficial transactions.

Knowing all the available ways of how to use Steam Points will allow you to maximize the benefits of this system. Whether you want to simply enhance your profile or make money from it, the possibilities are endless. The key is to be cautious, carefully choose items for resale, and select trustworthy sellers on third-party platforms.

Good luck with your endeavors, and may Steam Points become your reliable tool for both profile customization and earning!
