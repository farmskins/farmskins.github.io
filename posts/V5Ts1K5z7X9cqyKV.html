ZywOo

  • Rifles: Prefers the CS2 M4A1-S for its silenced precision, making it ideal for holding defensive positions without revealing his location. Rarely uses the AUG but keeps it in his loadout for maps requiring longer-range engagements.

  • Pistols: Relies heavily on the USP-S and Desert Eagle. The Deagle is his go-to for eco rounds, capable of turning the tide with one-shot kills.

  • SMGs: Rarely uses SMGs but includes the MP9 for bonus rounds or when economy is tight.

  • Situational Weapons: Keeps the FAMAS for low-buy rounds, recognizing its efficiency in holding angles.

s1mple

  • Rifles: Includes the M4A1-S in his loadout for rounds where he needs versatility, though he prioritizes snipers.

  • Pistols: Favors the P250 for its cost-effectiveness and the Desert Eagle for clutch plays.

  • SMGs: Rarely used, but includes the MP9 for aggressive pushes when the economy doesn’t allow for rifles or snipers.

  • Situational Weapons: Almost never includes shotguns but uses a stripped-down loadout to maximize utility purchases.

NiKo

  • Rifles: Chooses the CS2 M4A4 for its larger magazine, complementing his aggressive rifling style. Avoids scoped rifles like the AUG.

  • Snipers: Rarely uses snipers, focusing on his rifle aim instead.

  • Pistols: Relies on the Desert Eagle, a weapon he’s known to excel with, and keeps the USP-S for its accuracy.

  • SMGs: Prefers not to use SMGs, favoring rifles even in low-buy situations.

  • Situational Weapons: Occasionally experiments with the MAG-7 in maps with tight corridors, such as Inferno or Nuke.

  • Pistols: Opts for the CZ75-Auto for aggressive close-range encounters and the USP-S for standard rounds.

  • SMGs: Includes the MP5 for aggressive pushes on maps with narrow choke points.

  • Situational Weapons: Occasionally uses the MAG-7 for holding angles on maps like Mirage and Inferno.

Notable Loadout Trends

Pro players’ CT-side loadouts CS2 reflect strategic priorities and a deep understanding of the game’s evolving meta. One of the most prominent trends is the widespread preference for the M4A1-S, valued for its precision, silencer, and affordability. Players like ZywOo and ropz frequently choose this rifle for its effectiveness in holding angles and maintaining stealth. However, some players, such as NiKo, favor the M4A4 for its larger magazine, allowing for sustained firefights in aggressive scenarios.

Pistols also play a vital role in pro players’ arsenals, with the Desert Eagle being a universal favorite for its high damage and ability to shift the momentum in low-buy rounds. Players like NiKo and blameF often rely on it for clutch situations, showcasing its impact even when resources are scarce. Additionally, weapons like the P250 and CZ75-Auto are occasionally chosen for their cost-effectiveness and situational utility.

SMGs, while not as dominant, are used tactically in specific scenarios. The MP9 and MAC-10 are favored by players such as ropz for their mobility and effectiveness in close-range engagements, particularly in bonus rounds. However, these weapons are generally secondary to rifles or snipers.

Expensive or less versatile weapons, such as the AUG, M249, and Dual Berettas, are rarely seen in professional loadouts. Pro players tend to focus on economically efficient and reliable choices, ensuring they maintain flexibility in their strategies. Loadout decisions are often influenced by the team’s financial situation, with players prioritizing utility over niche weaponry and incorporating cost-effective options like the FAMAS or Scout when necessary.

Pro players’ CT-side CS2 loadouts reflect a balance between personal preferences and strategic needs, shaped by roles, economy, and the meta. By examining their choices, such as ZywOo’s precision rifles or s1mple’s sniper dominance, players can gain insights into how top-tier strategies influence weapon selection. Adapting these trends to your playstyle can help refine your approach and elevate your game.
