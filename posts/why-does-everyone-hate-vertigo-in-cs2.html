Why Does Everyone Hate Vertigo in CS2?

farmskins

Vertigo is one of the most discussed and controversial maps in the Counter-Strike community. Despite its long history and numerous revamps, many players and professionals still consider it unsuitable for competitive play. In this article, we will explore the reasons why Vertigo map in CS2 generates so much negativity and why even numerous changes have failed to salvage its reputation.

History and Updates of Vertigo

Vertigo first appeared in Counter-Strike 1.1 in 2001. Its creator, British designer Christopher Ott, developed the map using textures inspired by Half-Life. However, from the beginning, Vertigo did not gain popularity among players. Over the years, it has been repeatedly overhauled and adapted for competitive play, but even in its updated version for CS:GO, it has not managed to become a favorite map.

In 2019, Vertigo replaced the map Cache in the competitive pool, which triggered a wave of dissatisfaction within the community. Many players were disappointed by this decision, finding Vertigo uninteresting and awkward to play. One of the main issues with the map is its poorly designed structure, which forces players to repeat the same tactics, especially on bombsite A. As one popular player noted, "this map just sucks," and regardless of one's role on the map, it only brings frustration.

Problems with Vertigo

One of the key problems with CS2 Vertigo map lies in its design. There is a belief that the map was never intended for competitive play. It is too complex, convoluted, and does not provide players with the freedom to maneuver tactically. For example, the middle section of the map (the mid) is practically useless for terrorists, as it does not offer enough space for split attacks, making it less significant compared to other maps.

Another criticism is the gameplay on bombsite A. Terrorists often simply occupy the ramp and start spamming through smokes, turning the game into a retake shootout. This leads esports players to avoid playing on this map, turning matches into monotonous and uninteresting affairs.

Valve and Ignoring Player Feedback

The player and professional community has repeatedly expressed dissatisfaction with Vertigo, but Valve continues to ignore this feedback. Over the years, the map has undergone numerous revisions, but this has not improved its reputation. Some believe that the map remains in the game due to the stubbornness of the developers or because its creators have connections within the company's management.

Future of Vertigo

Despite all attempts to improve the map, it remains one of the least popular on the professional scene. In 2024, when Valve updated the game and made changes to Vertigo, the situation did not improve. As a result, Vertigo was played only once in two major tournaments. This clearly demonstrates that players prefer to avoid this map, despite all the developers' efforts.

It is likely that in the near future, Vertigo will be removed from the competitive CS2 map pool, with another, more successful map taking its place. One can only hope that this time Valve will listen to player opinions and make the right choice.

Conclusion

Vertigo is an example of a map that, despite all the developers' efforts, has failed to win over players. Its design, gameplay, and disregard for community feedback have made it one of the most disliked maps in Counter-Strike history. Whether it will ever be recognized as a good map is a big question, but for now, Vertigo remains one of Valve's most contentious decisions.
